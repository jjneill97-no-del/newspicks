News: AI-driven HBM demand is crowding out consumer/enterprise DRAM capacity at the big three memory makers; DDR4 contract prices are up over 50% in Q3 2026 and DDR5 is also climbing. Inventec, a major AI-server ODM, warned on July 16 that the memory crunch has now hit servers directly, with lead times exceeding 40 weeks.

Pick: Designer/manufacturer of custom high-performance DRAM and NAND memory modules for AI servers, edge computing and embedded systems -- not a commodity DRAM maker itself.

NOTE: shares the memory/semiconductor supply chain with existing open pick CAMT (packaging/metrology equipment) -- different link in the chain (module design/assembly vs. inspection tools), flagged as a mild concentration risk. UNP — Union Pacific Corporation just-so-story

Story: Farmer/trucker 'mega-blockades' across Mexico (Ciudad Juarez-El Paso crossing stranding ~38,000 truckloads, ~$1.45B in unrealized exports) have periodically snarled cross-border truck freight, theoretically diverting volume to rail.

Even if live, UNP/CPKC are diversified mega-cap railroads already running structural truck-to-rail conversion programs unrelated to this specific unrest; a short-lived disruption is immaterial to earnings, and 'railroads win when trucking is disrupted' is a reflexive association, not a sized causal chain.

STNG — Scorpio Tankers Inc. priced-in

TENB — Tenable Holdings, Inc. just-so-story

Story: Microsoft's July 2026 Patch Tuesday shipped a record 622 CVEs, including an actively-exploited SharePoint zero-day (CVE-2026-56164) that CISA gave federal agencies until July 17 to remediate.

Why killed: 'Buy vulnerability-management vendors after a big Patch Tuesday' is a reflexive, recurring association (happens most months to some degree), not a distinct causal chain -- exactly the first-association trap the process is meant to screen out. Enterprise security-spend cycles are too diffuse and slow-moving to time off one disclosure event.

Why killed: MP is the single most obvious, already-consensus rare-earth reshoring trade -- heavily covered for 2+ years and direct/first-order exposure (an actual rare-earth miner/processor) rather than second-order. The existing open pick METC (Ramaco Resources) already provides less-obvious exposure to the same G7 diversification tailwind via its Wyoming rare-earth project; today's IEA headline is logged as a reiteration for METC, not a new pick.
